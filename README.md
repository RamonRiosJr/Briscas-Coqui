Welcome to **Briscas**, the beloved Spanish trick-taking card game, carefully themed and optimized with the flavor of Puerto Rico! Whether you're playing 1v1 or teaming up with your *corillo* (2v2), Briscas is a battle of memory, strategy, and pure intuition.

---

## 🌴 How to Play Briscas

Briscas is traditionally played moving **counter-clockwise**, full of tension, bluffs, and tactical plays. Your main goal? Win tricks containing the most valuable cards, reach the target score, and secure the ultimate victory!

### 👥 Players

- **1 vs 1** (2 players)
- **Teams: 2 vs 2** (4 players)

### 🎴 The Deck & Card Values (*La Baraja*)

Briscas is played with a 40-card Spanish-suited deck consisting of four suits: *Oros (Coins), Copas (Cups), Espadas (Swords), and Bastos (Clubs)*.

Each suit has ten cards with specific point values. The rest are "worthless" in points, but essential for strategy!

| Card | Name | Points |
| :---: | :--- | :---: |
| **Ace (1)** | *As* | 11 Points |
| **3** | *Tres* | 10 Points |
| **King (12)** | *Rey* | 4 Points |
| **Knight/Horse (11)** | *Caballo* | 3 Points |
| **Jack (10)** | *Sota* | 2 Points |
| **7, 6, 5, 4, 2** | *Cartas Blancas* | 0 Points |

**Total points available per round: 120**

### 🎯 The Objective

Be the first player (or team) to win the target number of matches (commonly 1, 3, 5, or 7). You win a match by racking up **61 or more points** out of the possible 120 points in a single round. Tie at 60-60? Whoever takes the very last trick takes the crown! 👑

## 🃏 Gameplay Breakdown

### 1️⃣ The Deal (*El Reparto*)

The dealer shuffles and deals **three cards** counter-clockwise to each player.
The remaining stack of cards is placed face-down in the center to form the "Stock."
Finally, the dealer takes the top card of the stock, reveals it, and places it face-up partially underneath the deck. This card is **La Vira** (The Trump Card), which dictates the trump suit for the entire round.

### 2️⃣ The Action (*El Juego*)

- The player to the right of the dealer leads the very first trick.
- The game continues in a counter-clockwise direction.
- You can play **any** card on your turn (you are not forced to follow the suit of the lead card!).
- **Winning the Trick:** The highest card of the trump suit wins. If no trump was played, the highest card of the *lead suit* wins.
- The winner scoops the cards, adding them to their score pile.
- Then, everyone draws one replacement card from the stock (winner draws first), bringing everyone's hand back up to three cards. The winner of the trick leads the next one.

### 3️⃣ Swapping the Trump (*El Cambio*)

If you win a trick and there are still cards left in the stock, you can swap a specific low trump card from your hand with the face-up *Vira* under the deck!
*(Can only be done once per trick before you drop your card).*

- Have the **7 of Trumps**? Swap it for the face-up Ace, 3, King, Knight, or Jack.
- Have the **2 of Trumps**? Swap it for the face-up 4, 5, 6, or 7.

### 4️⃣ Black Hand (*Mano Negra* or *Brisculota*)

Got the **Ace, 3, and King of Trumps** all in your hand at once? Show 'em off! This is called the *Black Hand*, and it means you instantly win the round right then and there.

### 5️⃣ Scoring & Winning

Once the stock runs dry and the final cards are played, it's time to count points.
Add up the values of the cards you won in your tricks!

- **61+ points** = You win the round.
- **60 points each** = Winner of the last trick wins the round.
First to hit the target number of rounds wins the game! `¡Boricua, pa' que tú lo sepas!` 🇵🇷
